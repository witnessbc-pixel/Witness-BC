Who this training is for (and who it is not for)

For

  • Individuals who publish online and want repeatable privacy hygiene.
  • Writers/creators documenting events while minimizing accidental identification risk.
  • Anyone building “safe rewrite” habits (time/place/role generalization).

Not for

  • Legal advice, emergency situations, or case strategy.
  • Document review, evidence verification, or outcome prediction.
  • Publishing raw identifiers (names, addresses, IDs, faces/plates).

What you’ll learn (skills)

  • How re-identification happens (time + place + role + unique details).
  • How to rewrite without losing meaning (paraphrase, generalize, remove markers).
  • How to publish timelines safely (precision control and “pattern-first” framing).
  • How to separate what to store privately vs what is safe to publish publicly.
  • A repeatable pre-publish checklist you can run in minutes.
Training focuses on habits and drills. It does not provide case-specific guidance.

Preview drill (fictional example)

This preview is fictional for education. Goal: preserve meaning while removing identifying combinations.

Before (too identifying)
“On a specific date at 8:40am, I met a named role at an exact location. They used a unique phrase. I have screenshots and an email thread.”
After (de-identified)
“In early 2025, I raised concerns in a workplace setting. The response included dismissive language. Records exist but identifying details are not published here.”
  • Direct identifiers removed.
  • Time/place precision generalized.
  • Searchable quotes paraphrased.
  • Evidence referenced as privately retained.
